Free-form Improvisation

With Florida Studio Theatre’s Will Luera

This workshop explores the makings of the unique style of Big Bang free-form improvisation. This mindset is built on the concept of "follow the funny" and proposes that every single moment, beat, line and movement in a show can be deconstructed and used to lead you to the next scene. During this exploration and deconstruction of content, a language and pattern are established that help define form where previously there was no form. Students will review the tools that will enable them to create a unique free-form performance every time they hit the stage. We will review the ideas of listening to what the show wants, what can be funny, fluid editing and creating an experience that is a result of the space, the cast and the audience itself.

Will Luera is the Director of Improvisation at Florida Studio Theatre, Director of Big Bang Improv and serves as the Artistic Director Emeritus of ImprovBoston, Artistic Director of the Pilsen Improv Festival, Artistic Associate of the Chicago Improv Festival, Artistic Advisor to the Women in Comedy Festival and Part-time Faculty at Boston College. Will is co-creator of Healing Moments for Alzheimer’s, a non-profit organization that provides education, advocacy, and ministry for persons with dementia and their caregivers. Will tours globally as an improv and theater actor, director and instructor that performs and teaches regularly all across North America, South America, Europe and Asia.

Dumb...Found It!

With S&P’s Isaac Kessler

We've all been there. That feeling of emptiness on stage; when a “yes and” feels more like a “please end this now”. Eff that. In this workshop, taught by Isaac Kessler (2-MAN NO-SHOW/S&P), we will focus on empowering ourselves through commitment to physical and emotional play. Using techniques from Clown and a smorgasbord of other stuff, let's throw our beautiful bodies and hearts into the deep end, because in that is everything. Try not to think too hard about it -- it being anything. See? You're doing it again, you're thinking.

Isaac Kessler is an award winning comedian, actor, improviser, teacher, producer, and writer. He is a multiple Canadian Comedy Award nominee and has most recently been nominated as Best Male Improviser at the 2015 CCAs. Isaac is one half of improv/sketch sensation 2-MAN NO-SHOW, who did a full run at the 2010 Edinburgh Comedy Festival. It won Best Improv Troupe and Best Comedic Show, as well as Toronto’s Best Improv Troupe of 2013 & 2014 by NOW Magazine. Isaac is also a part of S&P. It was were selected as one of the top five improv troupes in North America by the acclaimed NYC Friars Comedy Club. Isaac also performs with the Wet the Hippo Collective in Los Angeles. Isaac studied Clown and Physical Theatre with the world-renowned Philippe Gaulier (mentor of Sacha Baron Cohen), Paola Coletto, John Gilkey, Dr. Brown (Philip Burgers), and Aitor Basauri of Spymonkey fame. He teaches improv at Second City and The Social Capital Theatre. He has recently completed a 5-month Lecoq-based Physical Theatre Acting Conservatory in Los Angeles under the tutelage of the incredible Paola Coletto.
